about me

Astronomy Graduate Student

Hi I’m Justine, an astronomy PhD. student at the University of British Columbia (UBC). I completed my honours BSc. at the University of Toronto in 2023 with a specialist in astronomy, major in physics and minor in mathematics. I completed my MSc. in astronomy at UBC in 2025.

My main research area of interest is planetary astrophysics. I’m interesting in planetary system formation and evolution, exoplanet detection and demographics, TNO observations and detection, orbit dynamics and more.

My undergraduate research was on Oort cloud formation and evolution in star clusters which involved creating N-body simulations of comets orbits a solar mass star throughout it's evolution in a birth cluster. My master’s thesis was on the TNO absolute magnitude distribution which I determined using TNOs detected by the CLASSY survey. I contributed to the detection of these TNOs by shifting and stacking the observation images and idenfitfying the object's position on multiple nights. Additionally, I’ve worked on observations of irregular moons of Jupiter and Saturn.

I’ve also worked as a teaching assistant for multiple courses at UBC, including ASTR507/407, 310, 102 and 101.
